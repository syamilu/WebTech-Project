Bedrooms in student residences are typically designed to accommodate one to four, although shared bedrooms exist in some cases. These rooms are usually spacious enough to accommodate essential furniture pieces such as beds, study desks, chairs, and wardrobes.

Most bedrooms feature standard single beds with comfortable mattresses and pillows. Bed linen, including sheets and blankets, is often provided or available for rent.

Privacy is paramount. Each compartment is equipped with its own curtain and curtain holder, serving as a cover for individual compartments
Recognizing the importance of academic activities, bedrooms often include study areas with desks and chairs. These spaces allow students to work on assignments, projects, or engage in self-study comfortably within the privacy of their rooms.
Bedrooms feature adequate lighting fixtures, including table lamps or overhead lights, to ensure students have a well-lit workspace and a cozy atmosphere. Windows in the rooms provide natural light and also allow for proper ventilation.
In today's digital age, access to a reliable internet connection is essential for students. Bedrooms in student residences often provide high-speed internet access, either wired or wireless, enabling students to stay connected and complete online tasks efficiently. Some residences may also offer additional technology provisions such as telephone lines or cable TV connections.
Wardrobes or closets are usually provided in bedrooms to enable students to organize their clothes and belongings efficiently. Additional storage solutions like shelves or drawers may also be available, depending on the residence.
